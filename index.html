05 Thoughts

What I'd do differently

Here's what I'd go back and fix.

Research depth
Real users, not hypothetical ones
The personas and funnel data were AI-generated to move fast. In a real project I'd run 5–8 interviews before concept exploration to ground the trust hypothesis in evidence, not intuition.
Dispute flow
Make it a first-class action
Two out of three testers missed the dispute action. A ghost button next to the primary CTA would surface it without undermining the recommendation — trust comes from the option to disagree.
Confidence model
Calibrate before scaling
"92% confident" is only meaningful if it's accurate 92% of the time. I'd want to see the calibration curve before exposing confidence numbers to users at scale — otherwise the label erodes trust instead of building it.
